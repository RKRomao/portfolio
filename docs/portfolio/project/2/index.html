NeoLAAC (Linha de Apoio ao Caloiro) is a digital platform designed by and for students at the Universidade da Beira Interior (UBI). Its primary goal is to act as a "digital mentor," simplifying the transition for incoming freshmen by centralizing academic, logistical, and social resources to reduce anxiety and disorientation.

Descrição Detalhada:

Overview and Purpose NeoLAAC is a comprehensive, centralized digital ecosystem specifically designed to support the transition and integration of new students (freshmen) at the Universidade da Beira Interior (UBI) in Covilhã. Acting as a "digital mentor," the platform aims to mitigate the logistical friction, information fragmentation, and spatial anxiety commonly experienced by newly arrived students. It unifies academic, logistical, and social resources into a single, mobile-first application. System Architecture From a technical standpoint, NeoLAAC is built on a robust, scalable microservices architecture. Orchestration: All external communication flows through an API Gateway/Orchestrator (laac-orq), which handles routing, security, and centralized auditing. Containerization: All components are packaged as Docker images, allowing for horizontal scaling. Data Persistence: It utilizes a "Database-per-Service" pattern, employing isolated MariaDB databases (e.g., a primary database and a separate one for calendar data) to prevent heavy traffic on academic schedules from degrading the performance of other services like real-time chat. Core Modules and Functionalities The platform is divided into several autonomous microservices that handle distinct functional areas: 1. Spatial Orientation and Infrastructure (map-service) Interactive Campus Map: Utilizes spatial data (GeoJSON) to provide interactive maps detailing faculties, building blocks, floors, and specific classrooms. Pedestrian Routing: Calculates step-by-step walking routes from a user's current GPS location to a specific classroom. Urban Mobility: Catalogs updated schedules, routes, and pricing for Covilhã's local public transportation networks. 2. Hybrid Support and Emergency Handling (chatbot-service, chat-service, emergency-service) Digital Mentor (Chatbot): Students can ask questions in natural language to an AI assistant to resolve common FAQ queries. Intelligent Triage & Escalation: The system monitors chats in real-time. If the bot detects critical keywords (e.g., "emergency," "hurt") or fails to understand the query, the incident is automatically and immediately escalated to human staff (Frontdesk). Emergency Features: Includes a "panic button" for immediate priority alerts and the ability to request direct emergency calls. Ticketing System: All incidents are managed via a ticketing system that allows staff to track the status (open, in progress, resolved) of student requests. 3. Academic Life and Logistics (academic-service, calendar-service) Academic Calendar: Syncs the general university calendar, including semester start/end dates and exam periods. Class Schedules: Allows students to view their enrolled courses, specific subjects, and daily class times and locations. Error Reporting: Users can submit tickets specifically to report scheduling or classroom errors. 4. Community and Social Engagement (post-service, events-service, feed-service) Unified Feed: Displays institutional news from UBI alongside social posts from the community. Student Organizations: Provides autonomous digital spaces for student groups (Núcleos, Tunas) to publish announcements, manage events, and share content. Guides & Perks: Hosts academic survival guides, Praxe guidelines, and a directory of verified local commercial partners offering student discounts. Security and Quality Standards NeoLAAC enforces strict non-functional requirements to ensure a safe and reliable environment: Security: Implements encrypted credentials (e.g., bcrypt), JWT-based authentication, and Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to manage permissions across different user profiles (Students, Teachers, Staff, Admins). Resilience: Features automated daily backups and health check endpoints (/health) for all microservices to automatically restart degraded instances. Performance: The API Orchestrator is designed to process standard requests in under 200 milliseconds, and the real-time chat maintains a latency of under 1 second.
